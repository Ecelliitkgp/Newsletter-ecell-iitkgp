How India is Promoting startups in this new age

To boost the innovation, entrepreneurship, and startup ecosystem, the government is focused on three aspects: "freeing entrepreneurship and innovation from government and bureaucratic silos; putting up institutional structures to foster innovation and handholding of youth innovators."

The government in the previous year has granted 28,000 patents. The government's help and support have also placed India in a better position in the Global Innovation Index and now stands at 46. Not only that, due to the growing startup ecosystem in India, job opportunity has increased manifold. There is now a greater demand for Engineers, Accountants, Management students than ever. Several Indian Grants and resources for Indian Startups are available in https://www.startupindia.gov.in/. Have a look at the website.

Crisps

DealShare, a social commerce startup focused on groceries and basics, has secured $165 million in its Series E round, putting it in the exclusive club of unicorns. This is the fifth Indian unicorn of the year. Jaipur-based DealShare is an inventory-driven platform that helps large cities manage their supply chains and logistics. It currently operates in ten states and over 100 cities, with plans to grow to twenty states and 300 cities and towns and targets the middle-income demographic through a community group buying model. The money raised in this round will be used to invest in technology and data science, as well as a ten-fold expansion of the company's logistics infrastructure and geographic reach. In addition, a large offline retail franchise network will be established.

Darwinbox, an HR tech platform, has more than tripled its valuation in a fresh $72 million fundraising round, making it a unicorn. It is the 84th Indian startup to be accepted into this elite group and the fourth so far this year. Darwinbox helps organisations manage their human resource needs—such as hiring and on-boarding, touchless attendance and employee management—digitally. It operates a cloud-based human resource management platform. Hundreds of firms, including Starbucks, Tokopedia, Zilingo and Kotak use the startup’s platform to handle onboarding of new hires and gaining visibility on their performance, attrition rates and establishing an ongoing feedback loop. The new funding follows a year of strong growth for Darwinbox.The company anticipates a 100% increase in its team and is preparing to enter the US market later this year.

Zomato Ltd, the food delivery service, announced on Friday that it will establish a wholly-owned non-banking financial corporation (NBFC) and invest in two startups. The NBFC's proposed paid-up capital is Rs 3 crore, while its allowed capital is Rs 10 crore. The wholly-owned subsidiary will need Reserve Bank of India’s approval to carry on the financial services business. The two investments, as well as the NBFC proposal, come at a time when many shareholders are concerned about new-age technology businesses like Zomato's viability.

In the next six months, Ola plans to open 500 dark storefronts across 20 locations as part of an aggressive push for its rapid commerce service, Ola Dash. The move comes at a time when other platforms, like Swiggy and Zomato, are looking to expand their presence in this fast-growing market. Others, such as Dunzo, and Zepto, are beefing up their rapid delivery offerings as well. Ola's quick-commerce focus is part of the company's strategy to portray itself as a super app ahead of a projected IPO (IPO). The company recently borrowed $500 million in debt to support its newer businesses, such as fast commerce and a platform for used automobile transactions.

Skeps

Skeps was founded by IIT Kharagpur grads Mayank Tewari and Prerit Srivastava, as well as Tushar Srivastava, an IIT Delhi alumni. The startup began as a blockchain marketplace where lenders could purchase and sell leads in a safe environment, while borrowers could keep confidentiality while looking for the suitable lender. Skeps here aspires to be the tech layer that connects banks, fintechs, and merchants in this expanding ecosystem. Skeps, which is backed by Bertelsmann India Investments and Accel, provides its technology stack to merchants and banks in order for them to surface Buy Now, Pay Later (BNPL) as part of the payments option rather than as a separate credit layer.
