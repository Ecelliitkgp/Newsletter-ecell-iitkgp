Potential in the Indian Tech Industry.

It is the time of massive startup growth in India. India is among Top 3 countries in the amount of investments attracted by its startups, along with the USA and China. It is at rank 48 in Global Innovation Index 2020 with more than 40,000 startups and developing Tech firms. 

Tech service is among one of the biggest contributors to economic growth in a developing country like India. With the growth of IT-enabled services,financial services,  e-commerce,internet of things (IOT), etc. Tech sector turns out to be roughly valued at $1 trillion, employing about 4.4 million people in the country.

India is gaining its position as the rising tech Powerhouse, with a very significant contribution in the Software Industry. With more than 624 million active Internet users it also serves one of the largest markets. With potential IT Hubs like Bangalore, we are competent enough to use the proficient developers we have and build the largest companies in the world.

The enormous growth in Tech Startups not only benefits the entrepreneurs but also leads to a boom in the Venture Capital industry. At present China has the maximum Venture Capitalist investors. Apparently,  Investors in China are facing great losses due to the fall in market value of the Chinese Tech sector. This will automatically drive all those Investors towards the market in India and USA.

Sigmoid

Sigmoid is a data analytics firm that specialises in using big data to develop technical solutions. It was founded by IIT Kharagpur alumni Lokesh Anand, Mayur Rustagi and Rahul Kumar Singh. Its "SigView" software provides real-time statistics to report on the effectiveness of adverts across numerous channels.

The company provides an enterprise data warehouse (EDW), which businesses may utilise to gain insights into their own organization's operation and efficiency by applying their own data. Sigmoid's technology can incorporate unstructured data and perform micro-segmentation, allowing it to make suggestions to its users. The technology may also evaluate streaming data to determine whether the user faces any hazards or opportunities.

Another element of Sigmoid is the data science-based interface. The user can enter their information into the platform, which will then design and validate a model. The model is based on data analytics and demand predictions. The Sigmoid platform will be used to track the company's success once the user has implemented the model.

Pocket FM

Pocket FM is an online marketplace for audiobooks and podcasts. It is founded by IIT-Kharagpur alumni Rohan Nayak, Prateek Dixit, and Nishanth Srinivas.
