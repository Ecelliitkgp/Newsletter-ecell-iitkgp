Government supports startups - scale up incubators:

The thought of being an entrepreneur and owning a startup is appealing, right? Who wouldn't want all that glory and fame of a successful company? But as the Minister of Electronics and IT Ashwini Vaishnaw, who himself has been an entrepreneur, rightfully said, starting out with an idea and transforming it into a company isn't easy; especially the initial risk phase. And this is where the government plans to step in, and that is what this week's Big Story is all about. 

So how does the government exactly want to do this, you ask? Well, the basic idea is to aid institutions that the startups collaborate with. It would assist startups and entrepreneurs during the most challenging stage, known as the risk stage, when angel investors, venture capitalists, and institutional funds may be hesitant to risk their time and money.

The current number of incubators and accelerators, which is around 200, is insufficient for a country with a population of over 130 million people, and thus the government will be scaling up the network of incubators and accelerators to multiples of 10x, 20x, 30x, and 40x in the next three years. All of them will be professionally handled and housed at institutions, industries, and other locations where they can be found.

In the event that this initiative of the government was announced,  the ‘Amrit Mahotsav Shri Shakti Challenge 2021' was also launched, on the occasion of Azadi Ka Amrit Mahotsav to encourage technology solutions developed by women entrepreneurs that facilitate women's safety and empowerment.

Overall, the motive seems to be to encourage the youth on the entrepreneurial journey; creating employment and making their mark on the society.

Bengaluru-based fintech startup Smallcase becomes the first Indian wealth management startup to be funded by Amazon. The e-commerce giant made its first investment of $40 million in Smallcase Technologies. Founded in 2015 by IIT Kharagpur graduates Rohan Gupta, Vasanth Kamath and Anugrah Srivastava the company provides users with weighted portfolios of stocks and exchange traded funds for retail investors to invest in using their personal existing demat accounts. The $40 million funding round was led by Faering Capital with existing investors Sequoia Capital and Blume Ventures also participating in this round. With this round, the fintech startup has raised $60 million to date. Read more.

 

Krishify is an agri-tech startup founded by IIT Kharagpur alumni Rajesh Ranjan, Avinash Kumar, and IIT Delhi alumnus Manish Agarwal, which is building a vernacular social networking platform for farmers to facilitate knowledge sharing and agri-trading. The company started out in April 2019 and received seed funding of $152,000 just a month after starting up from Orios Venture Partners and Applyhigh. Rajesh had identified that a new set of audiences was online in India, and wanted to create something for them. He chose the agri-community as he belonged to a farming family and agriculture was close to his heart. Initially, they spent a lot of time in rural areas to understand the farmers’ pain points. They decided to use technology to build transparency and efficiency in the whole process of the farmers’ lifecycle and connect them with the right people.to solve their problems. Besides being an online agri-community, the company also facilitates commerce. Read more.
