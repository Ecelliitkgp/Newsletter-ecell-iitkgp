Electric Vehicle Charging Infrastructure in India 

India is the fourth largest automobile market in the world. And the adoption of this market toward the EV sector is one of the major goals of the decade for the country. The FAME II programme, which aims to accelerate the adoption and manufacturing of electric vehicles in India, is forward-thinking and has sped up the transition to a more environmentally friendly automobile market. With this policy, India aims to become a hub for the EV sector by the end of this decade. According to current estimates, 80% of charging takes place in personal places such as the vehicle owner's home and, in some instances, the workplace.  

Evre, an electric vehicle charging infrastructure firm, and Park+, a parking solutions company, have announced cooperation in India to deploy 10,000 electric vehicle charging stations in the next two years. In a separate agreement, Hindustan Petroleum Corporation (HPCL), a state-owned company, promises to build 5,000 electric vehicle charging stations over the next three years. Also, TVS Motor Company, a global leader in two- and three-wheeler manufacturing, has formed a strategic relationship with Tata Power, India's largest integrated power company, to establish Electric Vehicle Charging Infrastructure (EVCI) across the country. Gramophone

Gramaphone is Indore based agritech startup founded by two IIT Kharagpur Alumni, Tauseef Khan and Nishant Mhatre. They have launched the Gramophone app in 2016 which has been able to map more than 2.5 million acres of agricultural land and has reached upto 8 lakh farmers. The founders of the startup were concerned about the lack of efficiency in the agricultural sector which provides jobs to more than 50% of the Indian population. The app has three major components, Gram Salah, Gram Uday and Gram Vyapar. Gram Salah, is for farm management and getting personalized agri based intelligence for the farmers. Gram Uday for providing access to the agri based units for the farmers and Gram Vyapar which increases the post harvest based trade linkages for the farmers. This revolutionary startup has been able to help the farmers to reduce their agri based inputs by upto 10-15% and helping in increasing the productivity of the crops by upto 50%. The Sandbox, a Hong Kong-based gaming platform majority-owned by blockchain game company Animoca Brands, has secured $93 million from investors led by SoftBank's Vision Fund 2. The Sandbox allows players to construct, control, and monetize their virtual gaming experiences on the Ethereum blockchain. As a result, the Sandbox coin, a cryptocurrency backed by Sandbox, has risen in value to almost $2 billion in market capitalization.
