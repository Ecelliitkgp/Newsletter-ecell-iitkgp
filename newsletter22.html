Investments on Web3.0 Startups

Web 3.0 startups are attracting a lot of interest from Indian venture capital firms, who see them as product developers for the future stage of the internet, which is defined by a decentralized online ecosystem built on blockchain technology.

Antler India, a subsidiary of Singapore-based venture capital platform Antler, has pledged to investing in 25-30 blockchain and Web 3.0 firms over the next 2-3 years.

Over the next three years, it expects to invest $100 million to $150 million in over 100 Indian firms, with up to $50 million dedicated to the Web 3.0 space.

Sequoia India has made roughly 20 investments in Web 3.0 firms, including Beta Finance, Clearpool, Coinshift, and Faze, through a combination of stock and token investments, in an exceptional year for the Web 3.0 market in India.The most exciting aspect is that the underlying blockchain infrastructure is finally approaching the point where it can enable high-volume, low-cost transactions. Blockchains have a lot of theoretical potential, but they're hard to use and grow in practise. This will begin to alter in 2021, and we anticipate that the trend will continue.

Non-financial tokens (NFTs), decentralised autonomous organisations (DAOs), incoming streams, and infrastructure are all examples of blockchain technologies.

Eggoz

IIT-Kharagpur alumni Abhishek Negi, Uttam Kumar, Aditya Singh, and Pankaj Pandey founded Gurugram-based Eggoz in 2017, which produces and sells brown, white, and Nutra eggs.

It all started when they left their cushy corporate jobs to do something big in the agriculture space. They realised that technology penetration in a space like agriculture is very low, but that it has a huge potential in a country like India as they explored options. Furthermore, despite the fact that eggs are the third-largest contributor to the Indian breakfast basket, 97 percent of all eggs consumed in India are consumed in an unpackaged, stale, unhygienic, and untraceable form.

"We saw this as a huge gap and an opportunity to build a brand of fresh eggs with deep integration with farmers," says Abhishek Negi, Eggoz's Co-Founder.

SOMETHING INTERESTING

Moneyball

In the film, Billy Beane, played by Brad Pitt, takes a chance on a new idea and makes significant changes to the way he selects players for the Oakland Athletics baseball team. His team loses some of its star players to teams with more money, and he must figure out how to put together a winning team in the meantime.

"Moneyball" is about much more than baseball. The important business lessons of adaptability, listening to new ideas, and changing the way things are usually done are all central to Pitt's and Beane's success in the film and in real life.
