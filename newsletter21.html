Cryptocurrency Bill 2021

During the current Winter Session, the government will introduce the long-awaited 'The Cryptocurrency and Regulation of Official Digital Currency Bill 2021' in Parliament. It's possible that a bill may be introduced in the following days. It is not on the Lok Sabha's updated 'List of Business' for November 30, 2021. After Cabinet agreement, Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman indicated in the Rajya Sabha that a new crypto law will be submitted in the House.

The crypto sector is looking for positive legislation that will allow investors and traders to invest and trade in cryptocurrency with certain constraints. Until now, there has been both positive and bad chatter about Crypto Bill. The Crypto Bill is one of several legislation set to be introduced in Parliament during the Winter Session. The crypto market in India was thrown into disarray and fear when the description of the cryptocurrency bill on the list was the same as previous year. The government's aim to ban private cryptocurrencies was reiterated.

There is a misunderstanding about what the government intends when it refers to private cryptocurrencies. Bitcoin, Ether, and other cryptocurrencies are available on both controlled and decentralized markets. All of these cryptos, on the other hand, are produced by developers or companies, not by governments.

Because there has been news concerning governments developing their own coin, the term "private cryptocurrencies" is intriguing. CBDCs, or Central Bank Digital Currencies, are the name for these cryptocurrencies. It's possible that these CBDCs may be categorized as public cryptocurrencies, while the rest will be classified as private cryptocurrencies. It'll be fascinating to see what falls under the category of private cryptocurrencies.

With a new $210 million round of funding, NoBroker becomes India's first proptech unicorn. The latest unicorn in the South Asian market is an Indian business that is transforming the way millions of people in the country lease or buy an apartment. People wanting to buy or lease an apartment can use NoBroker to avoid brokers and save money on brokerage fees. Bangalore, Delhi, Pune, and Hyderabad are among the key Indian cities where the startup operates. Over 7.5 million properties are listed on the startup's website, and over 16 million people have utilized its services.

India's First Privately Developed Cryogenic Rocket is Tested by Skyroot Aerospace. Skyroot Aerospace, a spacetech business based in Hyderabad, has successfully tested India's first privately constructed cryogenic rocket, the Dhawan 1. Cryogenic engines are rocket propulsion systems that utilise cryogenic propellants (temperatures below -150°C) as propellants. Liquid natural gas (LNG) and liquid oxygen power Dhawan 1. (LoX). The company claims to be the first Indian startup to sign a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) to gain access to its facilities and expertise. It employs more than 100 rocket propulsion engineers.

This movie is about a group of people who foresee the 2008 Housing Bubble and decided to “Short” the market instead of the regular people who went “Long” at that stage to incur losses.
