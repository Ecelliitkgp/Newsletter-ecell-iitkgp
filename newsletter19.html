Week's Prime

Success stories are often made of impossible dreams.

The story of the 43-year-old Paytm founder Vijay Shekhar Sharma, is no different. His journey from earning Rs 10,000 a month to becoming a billionaire has been flawless. Paytm began as a cellphone recharge service in 2010 and significantly came into recognition after Uber listed it as a rapid payment option in India. Paytm got a real boost in 2016 after the government brought demonetisation, highly encouraging digital payments.  By 2017, Vijay Shekhar Sharma had become India's youngest billionaire and has a current worth of 2.4 billion USD.

A landmark was created on Thursday with paytm’s listing. This was another watershed moment for India, as it demonstrated that public companies and major corporations are not the only ones that may become public and trade on stock exchanges. Sharma has always been optimistic about the startup ecosystem and the ‘India story’. 

The results till now, however, have been highly disappointing. 

Half an hour before the closing bell, the stock, which opened at a significant discount of 9%, hit the lower circuit of 20%. The stock concluded the day at Rs 1,564.15 a share, down 27.25 percent from the issue price of Rs 2,150. SoftBank, Ant Group, Alibaba, and Elevation Capital were among the early investors who profited from the IPO.

Paytm's IPO was the largest ever in India and the fourth largest for a fintech globally, with a valuation of roughly $2.5 billion. Paytm's $20 billion value proved difficult to justify in the public markets, according to analysts, due to its lack of a clear route to profitability.

Despite a rocky start on the stock exchanges, the fact that it had India's largest ever IPO after a decade-long journey is cause for joy.
