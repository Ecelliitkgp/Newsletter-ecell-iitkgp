THE NEED OF WOMEN ENTREPRENEURS

“We can only achieve great success if both halves of the society work together”.

If India is to achieve great heights of development and prosperity it can only happen with the active participation of women entrepreneurs and by encouraging women entrepreneurship.

Falguni Nayar’s Nykaa will launch its IPO very soon in the latter half of this year which has put the spotlight on her. This will be an iconic moment in the history of Indian startup ecosystem. With the launch of the IPO Nykaa will become the first woman led unicorn to be listed in stock exchange. According to the Google-Bain research from 2019, women owned 13.5-15.7 million businesses, or 20% of all businesses. According to an earlier estimate from the Sixth Economic Census (2013-14), women controlled 13.76 percent of businesses in India. Nykaa's IPO will prove to be a much needed push to the female entrepreneurs of India.

Big venture capital firms like Sequoia Capital promote the inclusion of women entrepreneurs in the ecosystem. Sequoia India Announces the Launch of the Spark Fellowship for India and Southeast Asia, as Well as Expanded Programs to Support Women Entrepreneurs. It believes that gender diversity is good for the society as well as for the economy and with such initiatives it tends to promote female entrepreneurs.

KGP has always encouraged the spirit of female entrepreneurship with many of our female alumnus holding top executive positions in big organizations as well as young startups. One such alumni Ms. S.L. Sivareena along with Mr. Subhadeep Mondal founded PregBuddy, India's first AI-enabled care continuum platform for the care of expecting mothers for which they were also featured in Forbes 30u30 Asia list.

Without a doubt Nykaa’s IPO launch is going to be a historic milestone in India’s startup ecosystem inspiring many more startups to become unicorns. Click here to read the full story.

MakeMyTrip exits ixigo's IPO with 8x returns.

MakeMyTrip has exited ixigo, an IPO-bound travel app, according to filings with the US Securities and Exchange Commission. The Nasdaq-listed business saw an 8x return on its first investment. MakeMyTrip had invested $4.8 million in ixigo at the start. The secondary deal was valued at $38.5 million, with investors including Trifecta Leaders Fund-1, Bay Capital, Malabar Investments, and White Oak buying out its position.

Edtech’s sensational rise in the pandemic

This year, edtech was one of India's top three funded sectors, with $1.9 billion raised over 80 deals. The majority of them were equity deals in the B2C learning arena, with market giants BYJU'S and Unacademy dominating. BYJU'S and Unacademy together received $1.44 billion in funding this year, accounting for approximately 76 percent of all edtech funding. In edtechs like UpGrad, Classplus, Teachmint, Quizizz, and many others, 2021 has seen some major growth-stage (Series B and C rounds) and late-stage (Series D and beyond) investments.

In the wake of the pandemic, Gameberry Labs grew from 2.2 million to 15 million monthly active users. Its viral Ludo Star game is the world's second-highest-grossing ludo game. Afsar Ahmad and Govind Agarwal, both IIT-KGP grads, founded Gameberry Labs in 2017 when online Ludo became popular in India. Mobile games that not only connected players from all over the world but also gave people games they could "grow old with," was their goal. Because "the digital form was satisfying and socially rich," Afsar and Govind, who had previously burned their fingers on multiple web-based products, decided to build an online ludo game (Ludo Star).
