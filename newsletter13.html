Evergrande Crisis and Indian Startup Ecosystem

China's Evergrande is the market's next looming problem, coming on the heels of the world's economic rollercoaster caused by the COVID-19 outbreak. The Evergrande crisis has enormous consequences for Indian markets, particularly the Indian startup environment, due to the interrelated nature of economies and markets.

Most experts feel that as India approaches middle-income status, support for the startup environment will only grow. Until now, foreign investors, both limited partners and general partners, had two major markets to choose from: India and China. However, the Chinese government and authorities have a negative attitude toward enterprises. There have been actions taken against the ride hailing app - Didi, microblogging platform Weibo, Alibaba, and food delivery giant Meituan. Many investors are scared of the Chinese market as a result of these activities. The Chinese government's crackdown has actually handed India a geopolitical advantage.

According to experts In India, there is a great sense of hope that has never existed before. India has established itself as a safe haven for foreign funds. This optimism may also be observed in India's recent unicorn craze. In 2021, we already have 27 new Indian unicorns, bringing the total number of active unicorns to around 61. Funds that were previously heavily invested in China and other emerging countries are now turning their attention to India. “Everyone used to claim that India's IT industry was booming, but when we look ahead to 2020, we see that everything is booming in India,” said Sreedhar Prasad, Advisor, Analyst, ex-KPMG, and ex-Kalaari Capital.
