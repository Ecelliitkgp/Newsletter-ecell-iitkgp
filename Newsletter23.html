Week's Prime

The launch of the India Blockchain Accelerator will expand the startup ecosystem in India.

The Telangana Blockchain District, which is backed by the state government and cryptocurrency unicorn CoinSwitch Kuber, has announced the introduction of the India Blockchain Accelerator, which aims to boost India's growing blockchain startup ecosystem. The Telangana Blockchain District announced the India Blockchain Accelerator in partnership with Lumos Labs, which has experience running blockchain-based open innovation programmes in India, after successfully launching and supporting eight innovative startups in the first edition of the T-Block Accelerator.

The goal of this accelerator is to provide expert mentoring and government accreditation to breakthrough deep-tech blockchain firms. This four-month accelerator will be available to early-stage Web2 and Web3 start-ups, as well as blockchain engineers, who have innovative blockchain solutions that solve real-world problems in one of several tracks. Fintech, entertainment, sustainability infrastructure and tools, agritech, logistics, and healthcare are among the tracks where solutions could be developed.

For their innovative ideas, the participants will be eligible for government certification, incubation space, ecosystem support, and go-to-market assistance. The start-ups may also be eligible for a $700,000 pre-seed/seed investment from investment partners Lightspeed and Woodstock Fund. These investments are conditional on the due diligence procedure and the investment thesis being compatible with the funds' and founders' right of refusal. The cryptotech sector has grown by over 39% in the previous five years, according to the NASSCOM Cryptotech Industry in India study. According to the Venture Intelligence Unicorn Tracker, India's technology and entrepreneurship sectors have experienced considerable development as well, with more than 230 cryptotech startups and 34 Indian firms achieving unicorn status in 2021 alone. As a result, the programme will be interleaved with multiple seminars, meetings, mentoring, and training programmes to ensure that start-ups are given the proper direction and resources to expand holistically.

OBEN EV

In August 2020, IIT Kharagpur and IIM Bangalore alumni, husband-wife duo Madhumita Agarwal and Dinkar Agarwal formed OBEN EV, an electric vehicle firm. Oben EV was formed with the goal of developing new electric vehicles in-house using "Made in India" components and building a global brand from India capable of supplying electric vehicles with elegant designs, premium quality, and dependable products to the rest of the globe. Oben's concept is to employ technology and innovation to create dependable, performance-driven, and connected electric vehicles that strike a balance between client desires, practicality, and cost. Oben EV has recently secured $1.5 million in a seed round led by We Founder Circle and other investors. We Founder Circle, Rakesh Somani, Co-founder of Life Element, Gaurav Juneja - Serial investor, Eminent Cars, Siddharth Shah, Managing Director, Rajesh Motors, and other important angels like Sumeet Pathak, Dr. Milan Modi, veteran technologist Dr. Aloknath participated in the seed round investment. The funds will be utilised to launch the company's product, accelerate new product development, and expand its experience centres. In March 2022, Oben EV is set to release its first made-in-India electric bike.
