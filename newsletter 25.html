6.5 Lakh Jobs by Indian Startups To Date

On January 6, citing the roadmap for CY22 DPIIT secretary Anurag Jain said “The figure comes to 11 jobs per startup,” addressing the achievements and initiatives undertaken by the government in the startup ecosystem. He stated that Indian startups have created 6.5 Lakh jobs till now.

Jain stated that India currently has 60K+ registered startups. “The figure comes to 11 jobs per startup and the revolution is transforming our country from a nation of job seekers to job givers.”

 

While DPIIT is yet to launch its annual report, some other facts related to the startup ecosystem discussed during the panel included:

  • India is the third-largest startup ecosystem in the world
  • 45% of all the registered startups were from Tier II and III cities
  • 45% of the companies were led by women entrepreneurs
  • DPIIT had also introduced a Funds Of Fund For Startup (FFS) of INR 10,000 Cr (approx $1.35 Bn) in 2016
  • Of the total FFS, INR 6,495 Cr (approx $935 Mn) has been already committed to Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs)
  • 40+ unicorns created in 2021 alone

Jain also added that the DPIIT is currently focussed on creating 20 Lakh new jobs in the ecosystem sector by officially registering 50K+ new startups over the next four years. To facilitate ‘ease of doing business’, the DPIIT, along with relevant departments, will also finalise the ecommerce and national retail trade policies.

In FY21, the Delhi-based consumer electronics firm boAt made a profit after taxes of INR 78.6 crore. This is an almost 61 percent increase over the profit of INR 48.85 crore recorded in FY20. The Mumbai-based business generated total revenue of INR 1,531 Cr in the financial year ended March 31, 2021, a tremendous increase of approximately 2X over FY20. The overall income of boAt is INR 704 crores. While the firm made a total revenue of INR 1,511 crore through operations, it made a revenue of INR 19.57 crore from other sources. During FY21, the company generated an EBIDTA of INR 127.1 crore. In terms of spending, boAt increased by 122 percent from INR 637.5 crore to INR 1,420 crore.

Fintech businesses have overwhelmingly praised the Reserve Bank of India's (RBI) new guidelines, which grant them access to borrower data held by credit bureaus. According to them, the new standards will result in faster processing of loan application. Credit bureau data will now be accessible to entities having a minimum net value of INR 2 crore. The new RBI standards will assist in making faster and more accurate credit judgments. In the lack of credit data, fintech businesses have relied on alternative indicators to measure creditworthiness, such as social network profiles and cell phone usage statistics.

From the 10th to the 16th of January, the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT) will host the Startup India Innovation Week. The virtual innovation event is intended to mark India's 75th year of independence and to demonstrate the breadth and depth of entrepreneurship across the country, according to a statement from the Ministry of Commerce and Industry. Enhancing market access options, conversations with industry experts, best practises by states, capacity building of enablers, reverse pitching by incubators, technological exhibitions, corporate linkages, and more will be covered during Startup India Innovation Week.

Reliance Industries Ltd's retail arm has purchased a 25.8% interest in Dunzo, a Google-backed hyperlocal delivery firm, extending Mukesh Ambani's foothold in the fast-growing grocery delivery business. Reliance Retail led a $240 million fundraising round in Dunzo, investing $200 million in the Bengaluru-based startup. The financing included existing investors Lightbox, Lightrock, 3L Capital, and Alteria Capital. Dunzo wants to utilise the new funds to speed up the delivery of goods through a network of micro warehouses, as well as grow its business-to-business (B2B) vertical to facilitate logistics for small merchants across Indian cities, according to the company.

Early Steps Academy

Founded by Sneha Biswas, an IIT Kharagpur alumna, this Ed-tech startup trains kids in 21st century skills such as critical thinking, communication, collaboration, and curiosity among learners aged 8-18 years. The startup works on a case-study based model and provides a holistic online platform. The platform engages students in over 1,000 subjects like bioengineering, entrepreneurship, cryptocurrency, NFTs, IPOs, space technology, and more, to hone skills in critical thinking, communication, collaboration, and curiosity. The Ed-Tech startup now enables learning for more than 300 students across seven countries in the world.

Rupifi, a 2020 founded digital payments startup focused on B2B payments, has raised $25 million in its first round of institutional funding led by Bessemer Venture Partners and Tiger Global. Existing investors Quona Capital and Ankur Capital joined the round, as did Better Capital, a Silicon Valley-based early-stage investor. Rupifi intends to use the funds raised to develop a complete B2B checkout product for marketplaces, as well as provide omnichannel mobile-first B2B payment solutions for merchants, distributors, and sellers.
