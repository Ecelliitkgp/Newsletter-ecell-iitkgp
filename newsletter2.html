THE CLEAN REVOLUTION

India is expected to have about 1.5 bn population by 2035, and thus with the increasing population, there will be a rising need for an energy source. According to the International Energy Agency (IEA), India is anticipated to be the world's largest energy consumer by 2040. India can no longer rely on imported fossil fuels to meet its rising needs. Our country now has 94.43 GW of installed renewable energy capacity to produce 408.5 GW (or half of the total demand) by 2030. Since 2014, India has received $42 billion in investment, with a projected total of $500 billion by 2028. With the growing demand for an hour in this industry, we can expect substantial corporate tycoons to get in.

Mukesh Ambani, Asia's richest man, announced a three-year investment plan of 750 billion rupees ($10.1 billion) in clean energy. Reliance Industries Ltd. is likely to spend 600 billion rupees on four “Giga factories” to do solar modules, hydrogen, fuel cells, and build a battery grid to store electricity. The Adani-led consortium is also stepping up its clean energy ambitions. Adani Green Energy Ltd. decided to buy SoftBank Group Corp.'s $3.5 billion renewable energy business in India to meet its objective of 25 gigawatts of renewable power capacity by 2025. NTPC, India's largest power provider, has committed to building India's most significant single domestic solar power plant in Kutch, with a capacity of 4.75 Gigawatt (GW). GAIL (India) Ltd has pledged to invest Rs 5,000 crore to develop a renewable energy portfolio of at least 1 gigawatt and to establish compressed biogas and ethanol plants as part of its efforts to diversify its business beyond natural gas.
