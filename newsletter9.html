Growing Investments For Mental Health Startups

In spite of being a major problem, Indian mental health startups have not received much funding over the years due to the stigma attached with mental health issues and a small number of trained professionals practicing in this field. However, this trend seems to be reversing, although slowly.

According to industry tracker Traxcn, Indian mental health startups have raised only $20 million between 2016 and 2020 while in the United States the same sector has received funding of about $556 million in the year of 2020 alone. However, with the COVID-19 outbreak bringing talks of mental health issues to the forefront, funding for this sector has reached $10.47 million as of August 2021, which is five times that of last year.

For several years startups aiming to connect patients with mental health professionals have failed to come to fruition due to the negative attitude associated with it and mental health issues being hard to identify but easy to dismiss and also the fear investors have that startups in this sector would not be easy to monetize as several consumers are not ready to pay for the services even though it is in high demand with 197.3 million people in India reporting some form of mental illness according to the medical journal Lancet.

While approaching investors, MindPeer’s Kanika Agarwal says that the lack of data and inability for the product to scale beyond tier 1 cities is a major concern raised and not only do they have to pitch their idea but also explain why previous startups have failed.

On the bright side, several mental health startups have received seed and Series A funding and are addressing investors’ concerns using AI and data-driven apps with Accel, Tiger Global, and Pi Ventures making major investments in the past year.
