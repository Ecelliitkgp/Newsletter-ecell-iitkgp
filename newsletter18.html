Current Global Fintech Landscape and the Lessons to be Learnt

Startup investors from all over the world are pumping money into fintech startups. However, through the third quarter of this year, global fintech startups raised $94.7 billion. One such influx of capital has given birth to a slew of new unicorns, as investors bet that the fintech industry as a whole will grow rapidly in the coming years.  Affirm, a public buy now, pay later (BNPL) company, exceeded investor expectations in the most recent quarter. Affirm wasn’t the only consumer lending company to produce better-than-expected results, SoFi posted Q3 net revenues of $270.0 million exceeding the expectations. Affirm and SoFi's strong results highlight consumers' appetite for debt in various forms. It's good news for startups in the market that are lending to consumers and their infrastructure providers.

 

Marqeta, a provider of developer-centric tools for credit and debit card issuing and payment processing, also reported better-than-expected quarterly results. Fintech infrastructure companies like Marqeta, Stripe and Plaid edge could serve as proof that an API-driven developer play can be a highly profitable business model. A crypto trading slowdown also had an impact on Coinbase, which operates the largest cryptocurrency exchange in the United States. The platform's user base shrank from 8.8 million in the second quarter to 7.4 million in the third. Simultaneously, trading volume dropped to $327 billion from $462 billion in the previous quarter.

As Coinbase does not have a secondary line of business to fall back on, its fortunes will rise and fall with the cryptocurrency market, which has historically been volatile and difficult to predict. Read More

Voice-overs with Speechmax

Founded by IIT Kharagpur alumni Anant Tripathi along with Chakrapani Mishra and Diwakar Chopra, Delhi-NCR based startup Speechmax helps content creators generate voice-overs in Hindi and English using their proprietary AI and Natural Language Processing Technlogy within a few seconds. What makes it different from other text-to-voice platforms is its natural and human-like voice-overs while other voice-overs tend to sound robotic. Founded in 2020 during the pandemic when people were jumping into the content creation zone and catapulted the demand for this product. With three voice artists currently Speechmax intends to expand to other vernacular as well as foreign languages in the future. Read more about their journey  here.
