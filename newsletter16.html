India’s SaaS Boom

Currently, unicorns are raining in India, with 32 startups already turning to the stage within ten months of 2021. Out of 32, 4 are SaaS startups (including Chargebee, Mindtickle, BrowserStack, and Innovaccer). But the Indian SaaS ecosystem has a new look after Project Superstar got successful on 22nd September, i.e., listing of Freshworks, the first Indian SaaS company to get listed on NASDAQ. Software as a Service (SaaS) is cloud-based software, and it is available to end-users via the internet. Today, SaaS applications are present in various fields, including accounting, budgeting, HR processes like hiring, performance management, travel, expense management, etc.

Various studies are suggesting that it is just the start of this growing industry in India. According to a report by Mckinsey and SaaSBOOMi, it is expected that by 2030 SaaS will create $1 trillion in value, making more than new half million jobs. Also share of the Indian SaaS market will rise from 2-3% to 4-6%, i.e., doubling the share. Another report by Google and KPMG expects the number to rise to 8%.
This boom can be attributed to the following factors:

  1. Covid-19 pandemic has been an excellent multiplier for SaaS companies as increasing digitalization and growing remote(WFH) culture has made a good business opportunity for SaaS companies.
  2. Indian SaaS companies have an inherent cost advantage due to lower overhead expenses and the country’s readily available low-cost, competent workforce. Over 100,000 SaaS developers and more technical expertise are projected to be accessible in India at a third of the cost in the United States.
  3. Also, India has well established IT services industry that provides backend support and an experienced talent pool to SaaS companies.
  4. India sees an overall development in Startups in general, with many people taking initiatives, government support, and many PE/VC firms ready to invest in upcoming ideas.
Overall, India is very well positioned to emerge as the leader in the SaaS industry, and this boom is just the start of its multi-decade growth.Read More
