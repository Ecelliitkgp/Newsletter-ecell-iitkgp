JULY MARKS A NEW HIGH FOR INDIAN ENTREPRENEURS

The Indian startup ecosystem has seen a watershed moment in July. There was a $6 billion capital inflow, which is roughly half of the amount raised in the first six months of this year.

Zomato became the first Indian unicorn to go public, with the addition of three new unicorns: Blackbuck, Droom, and Of Business. In 2021, there will be a total of 17 unicorns added.

The successful public listing of Zomato has also given investors renewed confidence that India can provide good exits, according to V Balakrishnan, Chairman of Exfinity Venture Partners.

The deal of the month was Swiggy's $1.2 billion funding round, which pits the foodtech unicorn against Zomato, which went public on July 23. Ola, OYO, Lenskart, ShareChat, Pine Labs, Digit Insurance, Delhivery, and PharmEasy were some other unicorns that cracked big deals. It's also encouraging to observe that 104 of the 133 agreements were in the early stages, indicating that good exits are possible for smaller funds that have invested in these early-stage companies.

The India narrative and its rising pace of digitization has been the underlying theme of capital inflows, proving appealing to both investors and tech entrepreneurs. PM Modi launches digital payment solution e-RUPI

e-RUPI is a cashless and contactless instrument for digital payment. It is a QR code or SMS string-based e-voucher, which is delivered to the mobile of the beneficiaries. The users of this seamless one-time payment mechanism will be able to redeem the voucher without a card, digital payments app or internet banking access, at the service provider.

OfBusiness

OfBusiness, a startup by IIT Kharagpur Alumni, Asish Mohapatra and his Colleagues from other colleges, has raised a fresh funding of $160 million led by SoftBank Vision Fund-II, valuing the company at $1.5 billion. Asish said he will use the new capital for acquisitions and strategic investments to gain greater control over the supply chain of its core segments like foodgrains, metals, plastics, petrochemicals, industrial chemicals, food grains, and construction materials are just a few examples. The business-to-business (B2B) commerce platform is now India's 18th unicorn firm, with a valuation of more than $1 billion. OfBusiness focuses on lending to small and medium enterprises, and through its NBFC Oxyzo Financial Services, it offers unsecured credit of up to Rs 2 crore. Read More.

murf.ai

Murf AI, is an AI-enabled voice-over startup founded by IIT Kharagpur alumni, Divyanshu Pandey and Sneha Roy. Murf AI is leveraging artificial intelligence to simplify spoken audio and make high-quality voiceovers accessible to everyone. It's a text-based voice-over tool that features hyper-realistic AI voices. Murf allows users to quickly produce convincing voiceovers for films and presentations without the use of any recording equipment. The tool also provides the capability to sync videos and presentations with the voiceover. Not just text to speech, users can also change their home recording to professional sounding AI voice and also edit their voice using text. It has recently raised $1.5 million in seed funding led by Elevation Capital. It will use this funding round for product development, hiring, and market expansion. Read more.
