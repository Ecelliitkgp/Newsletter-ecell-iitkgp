Overseas Direct Listing for Indian Companies

In an effort to alleviate the economic hardships endured by Indian companies and startups as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic, specifically the nationwide lockdown, the Government of India approved the direct listing of Indian companies on overseas stock exchanges, which was prohibited until 2019. But the government has not yet provided clarity on the issue, prompting startups to approach the government to accelerate this process and provide clarity on the unfinished reform agenda.

According to reports, the founders and CEOs of 22 leading startups and venture capital firms have written to Prime Minister Narendra Modi, urging the government to allow direct overseas listing of Indian companies. The reason stated was that startups must have access to international capital in order to compete on a level playing field with foreign technology conglomerates. According to the report, the total market capitalization of all companies listed in India is around $3 trillion, while the comparable figure for the United States is $50 trillion. The trend of setting up holding companies in Singapore in order to list their companies abroad could be avoided to a great extent if the government takes considerate decisions. They believe that the inflow of international capital would be very critical to accelerating the growth of Indian tech startups, international expansion and provide a level playing field with the foreign counterparts.

Bengaluru-based HappyCredit is an ecommerce and fintech startup that recently received funding from CRED's Kunal Shah, Goodwater Capital, and others. Cashfree Payments

Cashfree Payments, created by Reeju Datta, an IIT Kharagpur alumnus, and Akash Sinha, an IIIT Hyderabad alumnus, has won the ASSOCHAM Awards for Fintech & Digital Payments for 'Excellence in Payments'. Backed by State Bank of India and Y Combinator, Cashfree Payments is among the leading payment service providers in India processing transactions worth USD 20 Billion annually, helps businesses do bulk outbound payments with the upload of a simple excel file or through an API. Payouts are used by fast-growing internet companies like Dunzo, Nykaa, Fynd, Milaap to disburse wages to delivery partners, process instant refunds, pay marketplace vendors and online gaming companies like Rummy Circle to offer instant, 24x7 cashouts to players.

The attractiveness of Cashfree Payments was the clarity with which it recognized various digital payment issues and gave solutions that could be used in a variety of businesses. To win this prize, Cashfree Payments competed against 69 other companies, both startups and established businesses.

The Money Club

Founded by IIT Kharagpur friends, Manuraj Jain and Surajit Ray, The Money Club, a fintech business that has established an AI-based P2P group savings platform to enable the lower-middle-class population to save, borrow, and invest digitally, has received a $1.7 million investment from global VC fund SOSV and Venture Catalysts.

The Money Club is a safe and secure mobile platform for Peer-to-Peer Chit fund, Committee, or Beesi. It leverages AI algorithms to effectively underwrite high frequency, micro pooling of funds by studying user behaviors. You can join a Money Club with other like-minded people from all over India and start saving or investing money here. You can also borrow money that is multiples of what you have invested in times of need. Money Club connects you with a community of vetted people that can help you pool funds.
