Week's Prime

Fintech has become the hottest sector among Investors.

According to a Nasscom-PGA Labs report, Indian entrepreneurs raised more than $7 billion in funding in the fourth quarter of 2021 (October-December), up 18% from the third quarter, and 14 of these companies joined the ever-growing unicorn club. Unicorns are privately held companies with $1 billion or more market capitalization.

"Fintech continues to develop its legacy and penetration into the startup ecosystem. Growing infrastructure stack, innovative solutions, and new business models will continue to keep this sector in the limelight," the report said.

There have been various reasons for the growth of the Fintech Sector. The fintech market has benefited from the pandemic. With the onset of the pandemic, the fintech sector became the forefront of the country's financial inclusion struggle. The adoption of digital payments and lending surged. The market witnessed a 40% rise in digital transactions amid the lockdown. The new urge among the young generation to learn about the stock market and the money market has also contributed a lot to this growth.

Crisps

When it comes to pricing their shares in initial public offerings, the Securities and Exchange Board of India (Sebi) has urged that new-age technology businesses provide more rationale and transparency (IPOs). If the market regulator's proposals in a discussion paper are adopted, companies will be required to provide a very extensive explanation of how they priced their issue, compare it to pre-IPO share sales, and publish all pre-IPO investor presentations. The move follows a stock market crash in such companies as a result of the US Federal Reserve's and other central banks' hawkish tilt. In recent weeks, new-age tech stocks have dropped by an average of 50% from their highs

Prosus Ventures, Sistema Asia Fund, RingCentral Ventures, KDDI Open Innovation Fund, DG Daiwa Ventures, and Nexxus Global led a $35 million Series B financing for virtual event organiser Airmeet. Sequoia Capital India and Accel India, two previous supporters, also participated in the investment round. The new funds will be used to invest in R&D and scale the company's go-to-market function, according to Bengaluru-based business. Airmeet was created by Lalit Mangal, Manoj Singh, and Vinay Jaasti as a replacement for Zoom during the initial wave of the coronavirus pandemic. The platform is designed exclusively for events, with a backstage, stage, and social room, and it aids in the organisation of meetups, events, and workshops. The company claims to have helped over 120,000 event organisers stream 150 million people.

KGP SECTION

Founded by IIT Kharagpur alumni Ashish Mishra and Siddhant Bhomia, Krishi Network has roped in film actor Pankaj Tripathi as a brand ambassador. The actor has also invested in the start-up. The startup leverages the growing rural internet penetration to create a platform that eases information accessibility for farmers and enables them to generate higher profits from their land.

The startup said that the fresh funds will be used to expand its AI-based technology platform across India and grow rapidly.

The platform also has on-boarded agri brands, agri-input merchants, and other stakeholders in the agriculture supply chain.
