January 16 to be celebrated as National Startup Day

PM Narendra Modi declared January 16th as National Startup Day, giving a symbolic boost to India's burgeoning startup industry. EV charging infrastructure, defence manufacturing, semiconductor manufacturing offer, and drone industry were among the topics discussed during the Startup India Innovation Week, which was organised by the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT). "India is swiftly approaching the century of the unicorns," Modi added, noting that 42 firms became unicorns last year. "India's golden era of startups is about to begin." India currently has 82 unicorns. The startups presented to the Prime Minister on topics such as "growing from roots," "nudging the DNA," "from local to global," "technology of the future," "creating champions in manufacturing," and "sustainable development."

While speaking to over 150 companies at an event as part of the government's Amrit Mahotsav celebrations, Prime Minister Narendra Modi announced January 16th as "National Startup Day." Since 2016, the 16th of January has been a watershed moment in the Indian startup scene. The government of India, led by Modi, unveiled the Startup India Action Plan six years ago, with the goal of laying a strong foundation for the country's startup ecosystem. Because tomorrow is the sixth anniversary of the action plan's release, the 16th of January has been designated as National Startup Day.

The government has given state-owned businesses permission to offer property to private entities interested in establishing public charging stations. Land owned by government agencies could be offered to PSUs at a discounted rate of $1 per unit for the purpose of establishing public charging stations.. Owners can charge their electric vehicles utilising their current electrical connections at home or at work. The guidelines aim to minimise the country's emissions intensity by encouraging the complete electric vehicle ecosystem.

KGP SECTION

Kidex

KidEx, co-founded by IIT Kharagpur batchmates and friends Amritanshu Kumar, Kapish Saraf, and Gaurav Sengar, is a data-driven digital platform that provides classes and competitions for children in areas such as extracurriculars, co-curricular activities, life skills, and other areas to help them develop holistically. The startup helps children with different aspects of development like creating stop motion videos, learn sign language, karate, improve vocabulary, etc. It aims to develop the soft skills such as communication, creativity, imagination, social skills, critical thinking, EQ (emotional quotient), ability to use technology, understanding SOPs (standard operating procedures) etc, in children at a very young age. The Chandigarh-based four-wheeler electric vehicle (EV) startup EVage has acquired $28 million in seed funding from RedBlue Capital, a US-based venture capital firm. EVage has designed an in-house exoskeleton or modular structure to build customised vehicles. The business, which already supplies electric trucks to Amazon India for delivery services, will utilise this round of funding to finish its plant outside of Delhi this fiscal year and ramp up manufacturing. WayCool Foods, an agritech firm, has raised $117 million in a Series D fundraising round led by LightRock, LightBox, FMO, Lightsmith, the World Bank's International Finance Corporation, Redwood Equity Partners, and Gawa Capital. The new funds will be used to accelerate and utilise deeptech and automation to increase the company's efficiency by a factor of ten. Founded in July 2015 by Karthik Jayaraman and Sanjay Dasari, WayCool Foods buys roughly 320 varieties of fresh produce, as well as staples, dairy, and other food, from 3,500 small farmers across South India every day.

Refyne, an on-demand salary platform, has secured $82 million in a Series B investment led by Tiger Global. Digital Horizon is a new investor in the company. Existing investors QED Investors, DST Global partners, and others also took part. Refyne, which was launched last year by Chitresh Sharma and Apoorv Kumar, has partnered with over 150 companies, serving over 700,000 people. The investments will be used for mainly expanding the team, and for the product development. Till now, Refyne has raised a total of $106 million.
